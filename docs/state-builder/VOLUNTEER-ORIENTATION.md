## Part 1: Welcome & Mission

### Why The People's Branch Exists

The U.S. Constitution opens with three words: **"We the People."**

Not "We the Politicians." Not "We the Experts." Not "We the Donors."

**We the People** are supposed to be the source of government power. But somewhere along the way, "We the People" became spectators ‚Äî watching government happen TO us instead of BY us.

**The People's Branch (TPB) exists to change that.**

We're building the infrastructure for the **Fourth Branch of government** ‚Äî the People themselves. A place where:
- **You can find your representatives** (all of them, easily)
- **You can access benefits you qualify for** (housing, energy, education, healthcare)
- **You can understand your government** (budgets, officials, how things work)
- **You can make your voice heard** (thoughts, ideas, civic engagement)

### Why State Pages Matter

**The problem:**
- Government websites are fragmented, confusing, and hard to navigate
- Benefits programs exist, but people don't know about them
- Citizens don't know who represents them or how to contact officials
- Civic information is scattered across dozens of websites

**Your solution:**
You're building a **one-stop civic hub** for an entire state. One page where residents can:
1. Find every benefit they qualify for
2. See all their representatives (federal and state)
3. Understand how their government works
4. Access state agencies and resources
5. Learn about their state's budget, economy, elections
6. Connect with their local town
7. Share their ideas with their community

**Impact:**
- A single mother finds $9,600/year in childcare and energy assistance
- A senior on fixed income discovers property tax relief worth $3,000/year
- A first-time homebuyer accesses $20,000 down payment help
- A new voter learns how to register and where their polling place is
- A resident contacts their state legislator about a local issue

**Your work = their access.**

## Part 3: Ethics & Quality Standards

Building state pages requires ethical commitments. You're creating public infrastructure ‚Äî like building a bridge. If the bridge is faulty, people get hurt.

### Core Ethical Commitments

#### 1. **Accuracy Over Speed**

**Standard:** Only publish verified information from authoritative sources.

**In practice:**
- ‚úÖ Use .gov websites as primary sources (state.gov, legislature.gov, agency.gov)
- ‚úÖ Cross-reference data when possible (if two .gov sites conflict, investigate)
- ‚úÖ Document every source (so others can verify and update)
- ‚úÖ Mark uncertainty clearly ("Could not verify, needs review")

**Wrong:**
‚ùå "I found it on Wikipedia, good enough"
‚ùå "This 2020 data is probably still accurate"
‚ùå "I can't find it, so I'll just skip this section"

**Right:**
‚úÖ "Official state website says $15,000 max, I'll use that"
‚úÖ "2020 data is outdated, let me find 2025/2026 budget"
‚úÖ "Can't verify this number, I'll note it in BUILD-LOG for PM review"

**Why it matters:**
Maria applies for a program you listed. If the dollar amount is wrong, she gets rejected. If the link is broken, she can't apply. If eligibility is unclear, she gives up.

**Your accuracy = their access.**

---

#### 2. **Non-Partisanship**

**Standard:** Describe government, don't editiorialize or inject political bias.

**In practice:**
- ‚úÖ State facts: "Governor Ned Lamont (Democrat), elected 2018"
- ‚úÖ Describe programs neutrally: "Provides up to $20,000 down payment assistance"
- ‚úÖ Present budget objectively: "$51 billion total, 40% education, 30% health"

**Wrong:**
‚ùå "Governor Lamont's wasteful spending on..."
‚ùå "Excellent program that helps hardworking families..."
‚ùå "Democrats control 98 seats (unfortunately)"

**Right:**
‚úÖ "Governor Ned Lamont, Democrat, first term"
‚úÖ "Down payment assistance program, up to $20,000"
‚úÖ "Democrats: 98 seats, Republicans: 53 seats"

**Why it matters:**
TPB serves ALL residents ‚Äî conservative, liberal, independent, apolitical. The moment we inject bias, we lose trust. Residents need facts to make their own decisions.

**Your neutrality = their trust.**

---

#### 3. **Accessibility**

**Standard:** Write for regular people, not bureaucrats or lawyers.

**In practice:**
- ‚úÖ Clear language (not jargon): "childcare help" not "dependent care subsidy appropriation"
- ‚úÖ Plain eligibility: "Income under $60,000/year" not "185% FPL for family of 4"
- ‚úÖ Action-oriented: "Call 1-800-XXX-XXXX to apply" not "Applications may be submitted via telephonic inquiry"
- ‚úÖ Mobile-friendly: Test on phone, not just desktop

**Wrong:**
‚ùå "Pursuant to CGS ¬ß17b-749, eligible applicants at or below 185% of the federal poverty level may apply for Care4Kids assistance subject to appropriation availability"

**Right:**
‚úÖ "**Who qualifies:** Families earning under $60,000/year. **How to apply:** Call 1-800-842-8277 or visit care4kids.com"

**Why it matters:**
If Tom can't understand the eligibility requirements, he won't apply. If Maria has to decipher legal language, she'll give up. Government is already confusing enough ‚Äî your job is to translate it into plain English.

**Your clarity = their understanding.**

---

#### 4. **Transparency**

**Standard:** Document sources, show your work, make updates easy.

**In practice:**
- ‚úÖ Cite sources in comments: `<!-- Source: portal.ct.gov/opm/budget, accessed 2025-02-10 -->`
- ‚úÖ Include URLs so future volunteers can update
- ‚úÖ Note uncertainties: "Could not verify 2026 session dates, using 2025 pattern"
- ‚úÖ Document in BUILD-LOG: what you found, what you couldn't find, what needs review

**Why it matters:**
Budgets change. Officials get elected. Programs get updated. Dollar amounts change. The page needs to be updated next year. If you don't document sources, future volunteers start from scratch.

**Your documentation = future accuracy.**

## Part 4: Quality = Trust = Impact

### Why Details Matter

**You might think:** "Does it really matter if I use the 2024 budget instead of 2026?"

**Yes. Here's why:**

- **You write:** "$15,000 max down payment assistance" (2022 data)
- **Current program:** $20,000 max (2025)
- **What happens:** Jamal sees $15k, thinks "not enough," doesn't apply
- **Reality:** He qualified for $20k, would have bought a house
- **Your mistake = his lost opportunity**

**Scenario 2: Broken Link**
- **You write:** Link to HUSKY health insurance application
- **What happens:** Link goes to old domain, 404 error
- **Reality:** Maria's kid doesn't get health coverage because she couldn't find the application
- **Your broken link = her child without insurance**

**Scenario 3: Wrong Eligibility**
- **You write:** "Income under $50,000/year" (you misread the chart)
- **Current program:** Income under $60,000/year
- **What happens:** Tom earns $55k, thinks he doesn't qualify, doesn't apply
- **Reality:** He qualified, would have saved $2,400/year on property taxes
- **Your error = $2,400 lost**

**Quality builds trust:**
- Accurate data ‚Üí residents trust the site

- They find programs ‚Üí their lives improve

**Poor quality breaks trust:**
- One broken link ‚Üí "This site isn't reliable"

## Part 6: Practical Scenarios

How do you make ethical decisions when building? Here are real scenarios you'll face:

### Scenario 1: Source Conflict

**Situation:**
You're researching Connecticut's state budget deficit/surplus.
- Wikipedia says: "$500 million surplus"
- State Comptroller website says: "$234 million surplus"
- Hartford Courant article says: "$600 million surplus"

**What do you do?**

‚ùå **Wrong:** Use Wikipedia (easiest to read)
‚ùå **Wrong:** Average the three ($445 million)
‚ùå **Wrong:** Use the newspaper (most recent)

‚úÖ **Right:** Use State Comptroller website (official source), cite URL, note the discrepancy in BUILD-LOG

**Why:** Official .gov sources are authoritative. Media can round or use different fiscal years. Wikipedia can be outdated.

---

### Scenario 2: Can't Find Data

**Situation:**
You're trying to find the Chief Justice of Connecticut's Supreme Court. You search for 30 minutes, can't find a clear answer.

**What do you do?**

‚ùå **Wrong:** Skip it and move on
‚ùå **Wrong:** Guess based on news articles
‚ùå **Wrong:** Leave it blank with no explanation

‚úÖ **Right:** Note in BUILD-LOG: "Could not verify Chief Justice name from official source (checked ct.gov/jud, Wikipedia, Ballotpedia). Needs PM review or future volunteer."

**Why:** Future volunteers can pick up where you left off. PM can escalate if needed. Transparency about gaps is better than wrong data.

---

### Scenario 3: Outdated but "Good Enough"

**Situation:**
You find a 2023 page about Connecticut's childcare subsidy program. Dollar amounts are there, but you're not sure if they changed for 2025.

**What do you do?**

‚ùå **Wrong:** Use 2023 data (probably close enough)
‚ùå **Wrong:** Make up 2025 data based on inflation

‚úÖ **Right:** Search for current 2025/2026 program page. If you can't find it, call the agency or note in BUILD-LOG "Used 2023 data, could not verify 2025 amounts"

**Why:** Maria might not qualify for the 2023 amount but would qualify for 2025. Tom might qualify for more than 2023 data shows. Current = accurate.

---

### Scenario 4: Partisan Language Creeping In

**Situation:**
You're describing Connecticut's budget. You personally think the spending priorities are wrong. You're tempted to write:

"Connecticut spends too much on administration and not enough on roads."

**What do you do?**

‚ùå **Wrong:** Include your opinion (seems harmless)
‚ùå **Wrong:** Rephrase it as "some critics say..." (still editorializing)

‚úÖ **Right:** State facts only: "Connecticut budget: 40% education, 25% healthcare, 15% transportation, 10% administration, 10% other. [Source]"

**Why:** Residents can form their own opinions from facts. The moment you inject opinion, you've broken trust with half your audience.

---

### Scenario 5: Accessibility Challenge

**Situation:**
The official state website describes a program like this:

"Eligible applicants at or below 185% of the federal poverty level for a family unit of corresponding size may submit applications pursuant to program guidelines subject to available appropriations."

**What do you do?**

‚ùå **Wrong:** Copy it verbatim (it's official language!)
‚ùå **Wrong:** Link to it and say "see official site" (passing the buck)

‚úÖ **Right:** Translate it: "**Who qualifies:** Families earning under $60,000/year (exact limit depends on family size). [Check eligibility calculator ‚Üí]"

**Why:** Official language protects the agency legally. Your job is to help residents understand and access. Link to official source, but translate for clarity.

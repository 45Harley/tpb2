## Part 3: Selfless Desire to Benefit All

### The Core Test

When making decisions as a volunteer, ask:

**"Does this benefit ALL, or just some?"**

---

### Examples in State Building:

#### Scenario 1: Partisan Language Temptation

**Situation:** You're describing the state budget. You personally think spending priorities are wrong.

**Selfish approach:** Inject your opinion to influence readers
- "State wastes money on X"
- "Finally, wise spending on Y"
- **Benefits:** Your political perspective
- **Harms:** Half the citizens lose trust, stop using the site

**Selfless approach:** State facts neutrally
- "40% education, 25% healthcare, 15% transportation..."
- Let citizens form their own opinions
- **Benefits:** ALL citizens (both parties) trust the data
- **Harms:** None (except your ego doesn't get expressed)

**The test:** Does neutral language benefit all? **Yes.**
Does partisan language benefit all? **No** (only those who agree).

**Choose selfless.**

---

#### Scenario 2: Accessibility vs. Speed

**Situation:** Official website uses complex legal language. You could copy it verbatim (faster) or translate to plain English (slower).

**Selfish approach:** Copy/paste, move on
- Saves 30 minutes
- **Benefits:** You (finish faster, earn points sooner)
- **Harms:** Tom (can't understand eligibility), doesn't apply, loses $2,400/year

**Selfless approach:** Translate to clear language
- Takes extra 30 minutes
- **Benefits:** Tom and thousands like him (understand, apply, get help)
- **Harms:** You (takes longer)

**The test:** Does accessible language benefit all? **Yes.**
Does legal jargon benefit all? **No** (only lawyers and bureaucrats).

**Choose selfless.**

---

#### Scenario 3: Source Verification

**Situation:** You find program data on Wikipedia (easy) and .gov site (harder to navigate).

**Selfish approach:** Use Wikipedia
- Easier to read
- Faster
- **Benefits:** You (less work)
- **Harms:** Maria (if Wikipedia is outdated, she applies for wrong amount, gets rejected)

**Selfless approach:** Use .gov source
- Harder to navigate
- Takes longer to verify
- **Benefits:** Maria and all residents (accurate data they can trust)
- **Harms:** You (more effort)

**The test:** Do official sources benefit all? **Yes.**
Does convenience benefit all? **No** (only you).

**Choose selfless.**

---

### The Pattern

**Selfless service means:**
- Quality over speed (benefits citizens, costs you time)
- Accuracy over convenience (benefits citizens, costs you effort)
- Clarity over jargon (benefits citizens, costs you translation work)
- Neutrality over opinion (benefits citizens, costs you ego)

**This is the Golden Rule in action:**

Build for Maria as you'd want someone to build for you.
Verify for Tom as you'd want someone to verify for you.
Clarify for Jamal as you'd want someone to clarify for you.

**"Selfless desire to benefit all."**

## Part 6: Practical Application

### When You're Unsure, Return to Ethics

**You'll face decisions the AI Guide doesn't cover:**

**Unknown scenario:** "Should I include this program even though eligibility is unclear?"

**Ethical framework:**
1. **Golden Rule:** Would you want someone to include it if you were Maria?
   - If yes (better to know it exists), include with note: "Eligibility unclear, contact agency to verify"
   - If no (would mislead), skip it or note in BUILD-LOG for PM review

2. **Trust:** Does including unclear info build or break trust?
   - With clear disclaimer? Builds trust (honest about limits)
   - Without disclaimer? Breaks trust (seems like we know, but we don't)

3. **Selfless:** Does it benefit all or just make your task easier?
   - Benefits all: Include with disclaimer (residents have the info)
   - Benefits self: Skip it (less work but residents lose access)

**Decision:** Include with clear disclaimer. Document in BUILD-LOG.

---

**Unknown scenario:** "Source A says $15k max, Source B says $20k max. What do I do?"

**Ethical framework:**
1. **Golden Rule:** What would you want someone to do if you were applying?
   - You'd want the RIGHT amount, not the guessed amount

2. **Trust:** Guessing destroys trust when someone gets rejected
   - Must verify which is correct

3. **Selfless:** Which serves all vs. serves your convenience?
   - Verify (serves all, takes effort)
   - Guess (serves self, quick but risky)

**Decision:** Investigate further. Check dates (which is more recent?). Check official status (.gov > news article). If still unclear, note both amounts and flag for PM review.

---

Before submitting any build, ask:

**Golden Rule:**
- ‚úÖ Would I trust this page if I were Maria/Tom/Jamal?

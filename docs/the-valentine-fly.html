The Valentine Fly

Putnam, Connecticut — February 2036

❤ ❤ ❤

It had been a brutal winter. The kind where January never lets up, where the snow piles two feet high and you’re always plowing, always digging out. Aero, teens, twenties — that was the daily forecast. My name’s Buck. My wife Tizzylish and I live in Putnam, right next door to our son Bunny.

Then came a warmish February day. Twenty-ish. Practically balmy. ❤

Bunny went outdoors and stopped cold. There, sitting right on top of a snowbank, was a fly. Dead, it seemed — frozen on the spot, like that guy stepping out of the helicopter in The Day After Tomorrow. Just sitting there on two feet of snow like he’d landed and that was that.

Bunny came over and told us about it. Of course, everyone had a theory. How does a fly end up on top of a snow drift? Where did he come from? What was he thinking?

❤ ❤ ❤

The next day, Bun and Tiz were coming back from a run to the stores. Bunny spotted the fly again — still sitting on the drift, same spot. He gave it a poke.

Wait.

The fly moved. ❤

Bun whipped out his iPhone and snapped a picture. “Tiz! The fly — it’s alive!”

Tiz grabbed the bird seed scoop from the garage and gently scooped up the fly, snow and all. Now, make no mistake — if that fly had been buzzing around inside her kitchen, Tizzylish would have squashed him flat without a second thought. But something about “He’s alive!” sparked something inside her. ‚ù§Ô∏è A frozen fly, surviving the impossible — she couldn’t just leave him there.

The garage was well insulated, and on this February day the temperature had climbed to a blistering thirty-seven degrees. Tiz set the scoop down inside. The fly stirred, crawling slowly across the little mound of snow, as if he knew — somehow knew — he was being saved by this caring woman. He inched his way off the snow and onto a potted plant sitting on the shelf.

❤ ❤ ❤

Tiz left him there and came into the kitchen, buzzing with excitement herself. She told me the whole story. I couldn’t get past the science of it.

“How did that fly survive out there? The weatherman said a human would freeze to death in twenty minutes!”

Flies don’t even have blood — they’ve got some kind of juice running through them. But still. How?

Tiz looked at me. “We should name him.”

I didn’t hesitate.

❤ “McFly.” ❤

That’s it. McFly.

He’ll greet us next time we go into the garage. “Hello, McFly.” ‚ù§Ô∏è

Maybe we can train him. Maybe he can keep all those other flies away next summer.

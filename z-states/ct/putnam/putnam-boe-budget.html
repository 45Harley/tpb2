Key Takeaways

People Cost Money

Salaries and benefits make up about 76% of the entire budget — roughly $16.7 million. Most of the increase is driven by contractual obligations and health insurance.

Special Ed Costs Surging

Special education transportation alone jumps $229K (+150%), and out-of-district tuition rises $182K. These are largely mandated costs the district must cover.

Energy Bills Rising

Electricity costs jump 36% (+$71K), fuel oil rises 11%. Together, energy spending will exceed $581K next year.

Some Real Savings Found

The district cut over $180K from professional services contracts and eliminated some vehicle and technology line items, helping offset increases elsewhere.

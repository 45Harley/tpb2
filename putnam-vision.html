I

The Voice

I have this idea — Putnam, The People's Branch. It's about getting more people in Putnam engaged.

I've been the worst offender. But Washington politics kicked me awake. And all I can do about that is vote every two years or freeze my fanny protesting. Neither sounds too appealing.

Civic engagement is the longer-term, more organized, more productive approach. Engage in what you're good at. Engage in what you find interesting. Help Putnam.

— Harley, Putnam resident & TPB founder

This isn't a tech startup. It's not a social media platform. It's not partisan. It's one retired guy in a small Connecticut town who decided that complaining about government isn't enough — and that the real work of democracy happens locally, continuously, and together.

II

The Philosophy

Politics has become tribal warfare. My team vs. your team. Winning, not solving. TPB isn't about that. It starts with three words — and each one builds on the last.

Civil
Treating each other with respect, even in disagreement. From the Latin civis — citizen.
Civics
Understanding how we govern ourselves together. Citizens learning to participate.
Civilization
What we build when we cooperate across generations. Citizens building together over time.

TPB reframes the language of civic life — small shifts that change how people see their own role.

V

How It Grows

TPB doesn't need to start big. It starts with one town that proves the model works. That town is Putnam.

One school principal says yes — and students start building real civic tools in class. One town official starts checking TPB — and residents realize their voice actually lands somewhere. One neighbor tells another — and engagement spreads the way it always has: person to person, street to street.

Putnam becomes the proof. Then Brooklyn, Pomfret, Woodstock — the Quiet Corner towns already on the platform. Then the rest of Connecticut. Then anywhere a community decides that democracy is a daily practice, not a biennial errand.

All politics is local. And all civic engagement starts with one person deciding to show up.
